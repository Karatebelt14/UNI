About Us

Ateneo de Manila University traces its origins to the Escuela Municipal de Manila, a public primary school established by the Spanish colonial government in 1859. It was later taken over by the Jesuits in 1859 and renamed Ateneo Municipal de Manila. The institution underwent several transformations over the years, eventually evolving into Ateneo de Manila University in 1959, when it gained university status. Throughout its history, Ateneo has remained dedicated to providing quality education grounded in Jesuit values, shaping generations of leaders in the Philippines and beyond.

Ateneo de Manila University's pride stems from its renowned academic reputation and profound community impact. Rooted in excellence and service, Ateneo fosters critical thinking and leadership. Its alumni, committed to positive change across diverse fields, embody Ateneo's enduring influence. From business to politics, the Atenean spirit guides paths toward progress and justice, inspiring successive generations with its motto, "Lux in Domino."

MISSION

As a University, Ateneo de Manila upholds truth and applies it to human development and Earth's preservation;
As a Filipino University, Ateneo enriches Philippine culture, contributing to national development and the global community;
As a Catholic University, Ateneo forms persons-for-others, following Christ's example to promote service and justice, especially for the marginalized;
As a Jesuit University, Ateneo pursues Jesuit liberal education, fostering moral and intellectual virtues to see God in all things and serve humanity.

VISION

In the spirit of being Lux in Domino, Light in the Lord, Ateneo de Manila University will be a force for good in seeking innovative and sustainable solutions to society’s most pressing challenges. Rooted in its Filipino, Catholic, and Jesuit values, Ateneo will be a collaborative and engaged leader in the work of social transformation through education, formation, research, and social engagement.
